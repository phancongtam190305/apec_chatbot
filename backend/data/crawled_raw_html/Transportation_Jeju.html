How to get to Jeju

Flight

Located in the southern part of the Korean peninsula, Jeju island is easily accessible via numerous domestic flights.
Jeju International Airport (CJU) is conveniently located near downtown Jeju and is approximately a one-hour drive from ICC JEJU, the venue for the SOM2 and Related Meetings.

https://www.airport.co.kr/wwweng/index.do

How to get around Jeju

  • Bus, Taxi, and Rental Car

    SPublic city buses are the primary mode of transportation on the island. While Jeju has no subway or train system, taxis and rental cars are widely available, with many travelers choosing to rent a car for greater convenience.
    Rental car services are conveniently located near Jeju International Airport and Jeju International Ferry Terminal. Cars equipped with navigation systems are also available, making it easy to explore the island at a reasonable price.

    • City Bus Fare : KRW 1,200 (KRW 50 discount when using a transportation card)
    • Taxi Fare : Base fare of KRW 4,300, plus KRW 100 per 126 meters or 31 seconds
    • Rental Car Rates : Prices vary depending on the season, vehicle type, and rental company.
    • Website: https://bus.jeju.go.kr/?lang=en

  • Global Taxi

    Global taxis in Jeju are officially designated by the Jeju Special Self-Governing Province to help international travelers explore the island safely and comfortably. These professional drivers speak fluent English and offer helpful support throughout your trip—including assistance with itinerary planning.

    • Website: https://www.jeju.go.kr/globaltaxi/eng/index.htm

  • Jeju City Tour Bus

    Jeju City Tour Bus operates within Jeju City, taking visitors to some of the area’s most popular sights and scenic spots. A tour guide is on board and provides explanations in English and at least one other language, depending on their specialization.

    • Website: http://www.jejucitybus.com/en/
